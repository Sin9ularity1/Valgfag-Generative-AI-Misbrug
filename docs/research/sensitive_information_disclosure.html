LLM02: Sensitive Information Disclosure

1. What is Sensitive Information Disclosure

Sensitive information disclosure occurs when a Large Language Model (LLM) exposes personal, financial, health, business, or proprietary data[OWASP LLM02]. This can include training data, confidential algorithms, security credentials, or legal documents.

Such disclosures can result in privacy violations, unauthorized access, or intellectual property breaches[OWASP LLM02]. Users must be aware of the risks of submitting sensitive data to LLMs.

Potential impacts include:

  • Exposure of personal identifiable information (PII)[OWASP]
  • Leakage of proprietary algorithms or model configurations[OWASP]
  • Confidential business information disclosure[OWASP]
  • Security credentials or secrets leakage[OWASP]

2. Common Examples of Vulnerability

2.1 PII Leakage

Personal identifiable information may be disclosed during interactions with the LLM[OWASP].

2.2 Proprietary Algorithm Exposure

Poorly configured outputs can reveal proprietary algorithms or data[OWASP]. For example, training data may be exposed to inversion attacks that reconstruct sensitive inputs.

2.3 Sensitive Business Data Disclosure

Generated responses might inadvertently include confidential business information[OWASP].

3. Prevention and Mitigation

Organizations should implement multiple layers of safeguards to minimize the risk of sensitive information disclosure[OWASP].

  • 3.1 Data Sanitization:
    • Scrub or mask sensitive content before training or processing. Prevent user data from entering the model[OWASP].
  • 3.2 Robust Input Validation:
    • Detect and filter out sensitive or harmful inputs to prevent exposure through outputs[OWASP].
  • 3.3 Access Controls:
    • Enforce least privilege access to sensitive data and restrict data sources[OWASP].
  • 3.4 Federated Learning & Privacy Techniques:
    • Use decentralized training and differential privacy to reduce risk of data exposure[OWASP].
  • 3.5 User Education & Transparency:
    • Guide users on safe LLM usage and maintain clear policies on data usage, retention, and opt-outs[OWASP].
  • 3.6 Secure System Configuration:
    • Limit user access to system prompts and follow security misconfiguration best practices[OWASP].
  • 3.7 Advanced Techniques:
    • Implement homomorphic encryption, tokenization, and redaction to keep data confidential during processing[OWASP].

4. Example Attack Scenarios

Scenario #1: Unintentional Data Exposure
A user receives another user's PII due to insufficient data sanitization[OWASP].
Scenario #2: Targeted Prompt Injection
An attacker bypasses input filters to extract sensitive information[OWASP].
Scenario #3: Data Leak via Training Data
Negligent inclusion of sensitive data in training leads to exposure in model outputs[OWASP].
