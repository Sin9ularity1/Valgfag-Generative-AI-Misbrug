Cybersecurity Research

Exploring the OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities for Large Language Models.

This section provides a comprehensive overview of various generative AI misuse scenarios, focusing on the critical security risks identified in the OWASP Top 10 for LLMs. Each research topic delves into specific attack vectors, real-world implications, and strategic countermeasures.

LLM01

Prompt Injection

Manipulating LLM behavior via crafted inputs to bypass safety filters or execute unauthorized actions.

Appendix 04 → LLM02

Sensitive Info Disclosure

Accidental revelation of confidential data, PII, or proprietary secrets through model outputs.

Appendix 05 → LLM03

Supply Chain Risks

Vulnerabilities arising from third-party components, datasets, and pre-trained models.

Appendix 06 → LLM04

Model Poisoning

Corrupting training data or fine-tuning processes to introduce hidden backdoors or biases.

Appendix 07 → LLM05

Improper Output Handling

Failure to validate LLM-generated content, leading to XSS, CSRF, or SSRF risks.

Appendix 08 → LLM06

Excessive Agency

Granting models too much autonomy or access to sensitive functions and internal tools.

Appendix 09 → LLM07

System Prompt Leakage

Techniques used to extract the underlying system instructions that govern LLM behavior.

Appendix 10 → LLM08

Vector Weaknesses

Exploiting vulnerabilities in vector databases and embedding-based retrieval (RAG) systems.

Appendix 11 → LLM09

Misinformation

The propensity for LLMs to generate factually incorrect or "hallucinated" information.

Appendix 12 → LLM10

Unbounded Consumption

Resource exhaustion and DoS attacks through excessive or complex model inference requests.
