Cybersecurity Research

Exploring the OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities for Large Language Models.

This section provides a comprehensive overview of various generative AI misuse scenarios, focusing on the critical security risks identified in the OWASP Top 10 for LLMs. Each research topic delves into specific attack vectors, real-world implications, and strategic countermeasures.

LLM01

Prompt Injection

Manipulating LLM behavior via crafted inputs to bypass filters or execute unauthorized actions.

 LLM02

Sensitive Info Disclosure

Accidental revelation of confidential data, PII, or proprietary secrets through LLM outputs.

 LLM03

Supply Chain Risks

Vulnerabilities arising from third-party components, datasets, and pre-trained models.

 LLM04

Model Poisoning

Corrupting training data or fine-tuning processes to introduce backdoors or biases.

 LLM05

Improper Output Handling

Failure to validate LLM-generated content, leading to XSS, CSRF, or SSRF risks.

 LLM06

Excessive Agency

Granting LLMs too much autonomy or access to sensitive functions and tools.

 LLM07

System Prompt Leakage

Techniques used to extract the underlying instructions that govern LLM behavior.

 LLM08

Vector Weaknesses

Exploiting vulnerabilities in vector databases and embedding-based retrieval systems.

 LLM09

Misinformation

The propensity for LLMs to generate factually incorrect or "hallucinated" information.

 LLM10

Unbounded Consumption

Resource exhaustion and DoS attacks through excessive LLM inference requests.
