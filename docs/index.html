GenAI Vulnerabilities

Systematic research into the exploitation and defense of Large Language Models within modern cybersecurity frameworks.

1. About the Project

This elective project concerns the misuse of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) within IT security. The project investigates how modern AI systems can be exploited in attacks such as prompt injection, AI-supported phishing, and social engineering.

The purpose is to analyze concrete misuse scenarios and identify technical countermeasures with a focus on practical cybersecurity.

2. Motivation

As a student of IT security, I wanted to supplement my previous focus on traditional security principles with a newer threat area. Generative AI introduces new attack surfaces that are already actively exploited, making the topic relevant both academically and practically.

3. Project Purpose

This project investigates the misuse of generative AI in cybersecurity, focusing on concrete attack techniques such as prompt injection, AI-supported phishing, and social engineering.

The purpose is to identify central vulnerabilities in modern GenAI systems, analyze relevant attack vectors, and evaluate technical countermeasures with the aim of strengthening organizations' security in an AI-driven threat model.

4. Background and Delimitation

Developments in generative AI have raised broader concerns among researchers regarding responsible use and unknown long-term consequences. These discussions primarily fall under AI ethics and governance.

However, this project is delimited to the technical cybersecurity dimension and focuses specifically on how generative AI systems can be misused through concrete attacks such as prompt injection, social engineering, and automated phishing.

5. Problem Statement & Scope

Generative AI systems introduce new attack surfaces and amplify existing cyber threat vectors in ways that traditional security frameworks do not fully address. This project investigates both vulnerabilities within AI-driven applications and the misuse of AI as an offensive tool.

The detailed project aim, measurable objectives, and research timeline can be found in the technical appendix.

5.1 Project Scope & Methodology

This project systematically maps the GenAI attack surface by combining theoretical research with practical testing. The methodology focuses on identifying technical weaknesses through the following areas:

Technical Focus

  • Prompt Injection & Manipulation
  • AI-Generated Phishing & Social Engineering
  • Automated Reconnaissance & AI-Assisted Malware
  • Data Privacy & Identity Misuse (Deepfakes)

Research Method

  • Literature study of OWASP Top 10 for LLMs
  • Mapping of attack surfaces and vulnerabilities
  • Illustrative scenarios and practical proof-of-concepts
  • Continuous documentation via technical logbook
