GenAI Vulnerabilities

Systematic research into the exploitation and defense of Large Language Models within modern cybersecurity frameworks.

About the Project

This elective project concerns the misuse of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) within IT security. The project investigates how modern AI systems can be exploited in attacks such as prompt injection, AI-supported phishing, and social engineering.

The purpose is to analyze concrete misuse scenarios and identify technical countermeasures with a focus on practical cybersecurity.

Reader Note: This project involves several specialized technical terms (e.g., RAG, Vector Embeddings, Prompt Injection). For a full list of definitions, please refer to Appendix 1: Technical Glossary.

Motivation

As a student of IT security, I wanted to supplement my previous focus on traditional security principles with a newer threat area. Generative AI introduces new attack surfaces that are already actively exploited, making the topic relevant both academically and practically.

Purpose

This project investigates the misuse of generative AI in cybersecurity, focusing on concrete attack techniques such as prompt injection, AI-supported phishing, and social engineering.

The purpose is to identify central vulnerabilities in modern GenAI systems, analyze relevant attack vectors, and evaluate technical countermeasures with the aim of strengthening organizations' security in an AI-driven threat model.

Background and Delimitation

Developments in generative AI have raised broader concerns among researchers like Bengio, Hinton, and Hofstadter, particularly regarding responsible use and unknown long-term consequences. These discussions primarily fall under AI ethics and governance.

However, this project is delimited to the technical cybersecurity dimension and focuses specifically on how generative AI systems can be misused through concrete attacks such as prompt injection, social engineering, and automated phishing.

The purpose is not to assess existential risks, but to analyze practical security vulnerabilities and demonstrate realistic misuse scenarios that already pose a threat to organizations today.

Problem Statement

Generative AI systems introduce new attack surfaces and amplify existing cyber threat vectors in ways that traditional security frameworks do not fully address. This project investigates both vulnerabilities within AI-driven applications (based on the OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Models v2.0) and the misuse of AI as an offensive tool in modern cybersecurity contexts.

Focus Areas and Technologies

  • Analysis of AI-generated phishing
  • Social engineering using AI
  • AI-assisted malware
  • Prompt injection and manipulation
  • Deepfakes and identity misuse
  • Automated reconnaissance
  • Python and optionally local AI model for demonstration

Project Goals

  • Investigate how generative AI can be used in cyberattacks
  • Identify central threat vectors related to AI
  • Analyze concrete examples of AI-based misuse
  • Develop a simple local AI model as a demonstration tool (optional)
  • Conduct an in-depth analysis of one attack type
  • Discuss defensive strategies and countermeasures

Method

The project is carried out as a combination of literature study, technical analysis, and selected practical demonstrations. The focus is on understanding concrete attack techniques and evaluating defensive strategies.

  • Mapping of GenAI-related attack methods
  • Analysis based on OWASP Top 10 for LLMs
  • Illustrative scenarios and limited hands-on tests
  • Ongoing documentation via logbook
