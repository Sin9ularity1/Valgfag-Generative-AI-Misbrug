GenAI Vulnerabilities

Systematic research into the exploitation and defense of Large Language Models within modern cybersecurity frameworks.

1. About the Project

This elective project investigates the misuse of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) in cybersecurity, focusing on concrete attack techniques such as prompt injection, AI-supported phishing, and social engineering (OWASP Foundation, 2023).

The goal is to identify central vulnerabilities in modern GenAI systems, analyze relevant attack vectors, and evaluate technical countermeasures to strengthen security in an AI-driven threat model.

2. Motivation

As a student of IT security, I wanted to supplement my previous focus on traditional security principles with a newer threat area. Generative AI introduces new attack surfaces that are already actively exploited, making the topic relevant both academically and practically.

3. Background and Delimitation

Developments in generative AI have raised broader concerns regarding responsible use and long-term consequences. These discussions primarily fall under AI ethics and governance.

However, this project is delimited to the technical cybersecurity dimension and focuses specifically on how generative AI systems can be misused through concrete attacks such as prompt injection, social engineering, and automated phishing.

4. Problem Statement & Scope

Generative AI systems introduce new attack surfaces and amplify existing cyber threat vectors in ways that traditional security frameworks do not fully address. This project systematically maps these vulnerabilities through a combination of literature study of the OWASP Top 10 for LLMs (OWASP Foundation, 2023) and practical testing of attack vectors such as prompt injection and AI-assisted social engineering.
